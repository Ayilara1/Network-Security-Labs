# Network Security Policies and Risk Management

## Overview

This lab focuses on developing network security policies and performing a structured cybersecurity risk assessment using industry best practices. A hypothetical financial institution, **Apex Financial Services**, was used as the case study to identify critical assets, assess risks, and develop security policies for protecting sensitive information and maintaining regulatory compliance.

---

## Objectives

- Understand the importance of network security policies.
- Identify critical organizational assets.
- Perform threat, vulnerability, and risk assessments.
- Develop security policies for access control, data protection, and incident response.
- Apply governance principles to improve organizational security.

---

# Executive Summary

Effective security policies provide the foundation for protecting organizational assets, ensuring regulatory compliance, and reducing cyber risk. During this lab, a comprehensive security framework was developed for **Apex Financial Services**, focusing on governance, risk management, and continuous security improvement.

# Lab 2 – Risk Assessment

## Objective

Identify organizational assets, threats, vulnerabilities, and calculate cybersecurity risk.

---

## Critical Assets Identified

### Asset A-1

**Customer Personally Identifiable Information (PII)**

Examples include:

- Customer identities
- Financial records
- Transaction history
- Account information

---

### Asset A-2

**Transaction Processing Systems**

Includes:

- Servers
- Databases
- Network infrastructure
- Payment processing systems

---

### Asset A-3

**Intellectual Property**

Includes:

- Source code
- Security documentation
- Proprietary trading algorithms

---

## Threat Analysis

Threats evaluated included:

- Phishing attacks
- Ransomware
- Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)
- Insider threats
- Zero-day exploits
- Unpatched software vulnerabilities

---

## Risk Matrix

| Threat | Likelihood | Impact | Risk Rating | Treatment |
|---------|------------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Phishing & Ransomware | High | Catastrophic | Critical | MFA, Security Awareness Training |
| DDoS Attack | Medium | High | High | DDoS Protection Services |
| Zero-Day Vulnerabilities | Medium | Catastrophic | High | Patch Management |
| Insider Threat | Low | High | Medium | Least Privilege |

# Lab 3 – Access Control Policy (ACP)

## Objective

Develop an access control framework based on the Principle of Least Privilege.

### Policy Highlights

### Authentication

- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Strong password requirements
- Secure remote authentication

---

### Authorization

- Least Privilege
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Quarterly access reviews

---

### Session Management

- Automatic session timeout after **15 minutes**
- Re-authentication required for privileged access

# Lab 7 – Policy Review and Continuous Improvement

## Objective

Ensure policies remain effective as threats evolve.

### Peer Recommendations

- Introduce measurable security metrics.
- Schedule biannual policy reviews.
- Improve threat intelligence integration.
- Automate security controls where possible.

### Reflection

This exercise demonstrated that cybersecurity governance is a continuous improvement process rather than a one-time implementation. Policies should evolve alongside business objectives, emerging technologies, and the changing threat landscape.
